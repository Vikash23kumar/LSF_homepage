About Us

Awareness is like the Sun. When it shines on things, they are transformed. Learning Space Foundation strongly believes that the first step in creating a better society is empowering our future citizens. We work towards empowerment of marginalized children and youth by raising awareness and educating them about health, safety and social issues and building necessary skills to enable them overcome success barriers. We work with other NGOs and create collaborative synergy by providing our services in program management, training the students, teachers and facilitating leadership workshops for organizational effectiveness.

Vision

Learning Space envisions a world where all young people stay healthy, treat each other and the environment with respect, dignity, become responsible and fulfilled members of the society and live in peace and harmony.

Mission

To empower children and youth by building necessary skills that help them to be safe, happy and healthy. To develop, promote and encourage uplifting social and educational processes for students and teachers.

Story of our success

Learning Space started in 2014 as a charitable organization by conviction. Our journey started as training cum implementation partner for a 2-year Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) project for 20 Rainbow Homes in the twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad which aimed at improving learning abilities of ~1300 children taking shelter in these orphanages using technology aided teaching and learning. Later we handled training and implementation of another STEM project for about 12,000 less privileged students studying at 88 government primary, upper-primary and high schools of Nampally and Khairatabad Mandals in Hyderabad. These projects were done in collaboration with United Way Hyderabad, Bodhaguru and some IT companies. We also worked with some other NGOs such as Association for Promoting Social Action (APSA) and Suma Niketan. Learning Space Foundation is a registered as a public charitable trust in Dec, 2018. While we were working with those organizations and schools, we got to know about some unfortunate incidents where children were being sexually abused, bullying and harassing other students, eve teasing, some children endangering their future by making wrong decisions etc. One such example is Chotu (name changed to protect privacy). He is an intelligent youth, but unaware of the child marriage act and POCSO, married a minor girl against elders' wish and landed in jail. Most of them being first generation learners, they have little knowledge about law and how their actions and decisions can destroy their dignity and future. It feels devastated if we think about what happens to their self-concept. Studies reveal that teenagers with low self-esteem have low confidence, feel insecure and may develop anti-social attitude. They need to be given awareness on health and hygiene as well. It is a common sight during breaks, that kids eat packaged junk foods which are very harmful for growing children. As it is a known fact that social and creative learning happens only when a child is healthy and safe. So, it became our immediate priority to create awareness among children, teachers and communities about health and safety so that children can explore, learn and grow.
